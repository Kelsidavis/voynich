## Interpretation

### Evidence FOR Basque Botanical Connection

1. **"lor" = flower** (43 occurrences)
   - Direct phonetic match to Basque "lore"
   - Takes Voynich case markers (loraiin)

2. **"olo" = oat** (4 occurrences)
   - Exact match to Basque "olo"
   - Forms compounds (oloeorain)

3. **"ota" = gorse** (2 occurrences)
   - Exact match to Basque "ota"
   - Specific plant identification

4. **Compound formation**
   - "oloeorain" shows Basque-like compounding
   - "loraiin" shows Basque root + Voynich grammar

### Evidence FOR Sister Language (not Basque itself)

1. **Missing core vocabulary**
   - "hosto" (leaf) absent → "daiin" instead
   - "zuhaitz" (tree) absent → "ar" instead?
   - "belar" (herb) absent

2. **Different dominant terms**
   - Basque flower = "lore" → Voynich has both "lor" AND "chol"
   - Suggests vocabulary divergence

3. **Voynich grammar applied**
   - Basque roots take Voynich suffixes (-aiin, -al, -or)
   - NOT Basque suffixes (-ak, -ari, -ekin)
