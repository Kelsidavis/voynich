# Etruscan Connection Investigation

## Background

Etruscan was a pre-Indo-European language spoken in ancient Italy (Etruria/Tuscany) before Roman conquest. Like Basque, it is classified as a language isolate with no proven relatives.

### Why Test Etruscan?

1. **Pre-IE isolate** - Same category as Basque
2. **Geographic proximity** - Northern Italy, near Voynich's proposed origin
3. **Historical overlap** - Etruscan survived into early Roman period
4. **Proposed Basque connection** - Some scholars suggest distant relationship

## The Etruscan-Basque Hypothesis

### Proposed Historical Connection

Some scholars have suggested Etruscan and Basque share a common pre-IE ancestor:
- Both are language isolates
- Both survived into historical periods
- Geographic distribution along Mediterranean
- Some proposed cognates (controversial)

### Proposed Cognates (Controversial)

| Etruscan | Basque | Meaning | In Voynich? |
|----------|--------|---------|-------------|
| neft | neba? | grandson/brother | No |
| clan | ? | son | No |
| ais | - | god | 1? (weak) |

**These proposed cognates are highly disputed by mainstream linguistics.**

### If Etruscan-Basque Connection Were Real

If Voynich were related to both Basque AND Etruscan (through a common ancestor), we would expect:
- Shared vocabulary with both
- Possibly more Etruscan matches than other IE languages

**What we actually find:**
- **16-19 Basque matches**
- **1-2 weak Etruscan matches**
- Pattern suggests **Basque relationship, not Etruscan**

## Analysis

### Why So Few Etruscan Matches?

| Explanation | Plausibility |
|-------------|--------------|
| Etruscan corpus is limited | HIGH - only ~13,000 inscriptions |
| Voynich unrelated to Etruscan | HIGH - evidence supports this |
| Etruscan died too early | MODERATE - extinct by ~1st c. CE |
| Different branch of pre-IE | POSSIBLE - if related at all |

### Etruscan Limitations

1. **Limited vocabulary known** - Mostly funerary/religious inscriptions
2. **No herbal/medical texts** - Can't compare semantic domains
3. **No living descendants** - Can't verify reconstructions
4. **Extinct by Roman period** - 1400+ years before Voynich

### Basque Advantages

1. **Living language** - Vocabulary well documented
2. **Semantic domain matches** - Botanical, body, medical
3. **Morphological evidence** - Basque roots + Voynich suffixes
4. **Survived to present** - Could have sister language into medieval period

### Final Assessment

| Hypothesis | Probability | Evidence |

**Basque remains the strongest pre-IE candidate** with 10x more evidence.
