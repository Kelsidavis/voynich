## Methodology

1. **Corpus extraction**: All unique words extracted from EVA transcription

3. **Verification**: Each pattern tested for corpus-wide consistency
4. **Confidence rating**: Based on evidence strength, not assumption

### 3.2 Words That Actually Exist

| Word | Count | Claimed Polish | Evidence |
|------|-------|----------------|----------|
| dar | 297 | dać (to give) | Possible, but takes Voynich suffixes not Polish |
| dal | 243 | dał (he gave) | Patterns identically to 'dar' - variant? |
| chor | 206 | chory (sick) | No Polish case forms appear |
| chory | 10 | chory (sick) | Could be chor + -y suffix |
| dam | 89 | dam (I will give) | Or [d] + [-am] suffix |
| dan | 16 | dany (given) | Or [d] + [-an] suffix |

**Critical problem**: 'dar' and 'dal' take IDENTICAL suffixes:
- dary: 22, daly: 28
- daraiin: 2, dalaiin: 3

If they were infinitive/past tense, they should appear in different contexts.
They behave like spelling variants, NOT grammatical forms.

### 3.3 The 'ol' Problem

Previous claim: 'ol' = Latin 'oleum' (oil)

Corpus reality:
- ol- as prefix: 1,587 tokens
- -ol as suffix: 2,962 tokens (DOUBLE)

Words like 'chol', 'shol', 'sheol' are [root]-ol, NOT Latin declension.
'-ol' is a VOYNICH SUFFIX, not Latin vocabulary.
