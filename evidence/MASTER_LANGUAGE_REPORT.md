## Part 8: The Copy Hypothesis (NEW)

### Key Distinction

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Author** | The person who composed the original text |
| **Scribe/Copyist** | The person who copied it onto the surviving vellum |

**These may be different people, separated by decades or centuries.**

### Evidence Supporting the Copy Hypothesis

#### 1. Vellum vs Text Dating

| What We Know | Implication |
|--------------|-------------|
| Vellum carbon-dated to 1404-1438 | The MATERIAL is early 15th century |
| Text could be older | Writing copied onto new vellum |
| No ink dating available | Cannot date the actual writing |

**The vellum dates the COPY, not the original composition.**

#### 2. Linguistic Archaism

The Pre-IE vocabulary pattern fits better with an **older original**:
- 15th century: Pre-IE languages nearly extinct in Western Europe
- 13th-14th century: More plausible for living Pre-IE community
- 12th century or earlier: Even more plausible

#### 3. Scribal Indicators in the Manuscript

| Feature | Observation | Copy Indicator? |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|
| Consistent hand | Single scribe for most | YES - professional copy |
| Some hand variations | Multiple scribes? | YES - copying project |
| Marginalia | Few additions | YES - sacred/fixed text |
| Corrections | Some visible | YES - copying errors fixed |
| Consistent format | Throughout manuscript | YES - following exemplar |

### Revised Timeline Hypothesis

```
ORIGINAL COMPOSITION
│
│  11th-13th century?
│  - Pre-IE speaking community still active
│  - Original author = native speaker/healer
│  - Text composed in living language
│
▼
FIRST COPY(?)
│
│  13th-14th century?
│  - Language declining
│  - Copied for preservation

▼

```

### Revised Author vs Copyist Profile

| Trait | Original Author (11th-14th c.?) | 15th c. Copyist |
|-------|---------------------------------|-----------------|
| Language | Native Pre-IE speaker | May not understand |
| Purpose | Practical use | Preservation |
| Knowledge | Expert healer | Professional scribe |
| Location | Pre-IE community | Northern Italy? |

### Why This Matters

1. **Explains Linguistic Archaism**: Pre-IE language more plausible in 12th-13th century
2. **Explains Preservation Motive**: Ancient text valued for knowledge/heritage
3. **Explains Scribal "Errors"**: Copying by unfamiliar scribe
4. **Explains Illustration Updates**: Redrawn in 15th century style

### Key Insight

**The Voynich manuscript may preserve a text 200-300 years older than the vellum it's written on.**

This would make it one of the last surviving records of a Pre-Indo-European language that was already dead or dying when the surviving copy was made.
