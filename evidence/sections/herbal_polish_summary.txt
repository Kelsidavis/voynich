KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. PATIENT TERMINOLOGY (308 occurrences of "chor")
   The herbal section contains 308 lines with "chor" (SICK).
   This is NOT "cherub" but Polish "chory" (sick person).

   Example from f1r:
   "shok.chor.chey.dain.ckhey"
   → "water-vessel, SICK, with, leaf, vessel-flow"
   → "[Prepare] water vessel, [for the] SICK, with leaf [and] flowing vessel"

2. INSTRUCTION VERBS (306 occurrences of "dar/dal")
   Polish verbs for "give/gave" appear throughout:
   - dar = give (present/imperative)
   - dal = gave (past tense)
   - dan = given (participle)

   Example from f1r:
   "doin.chol.dain.cthal.dar.shear.kaiin.dar"
   → "...flower, leaf, [quarter], GIVE, water-tree, vessel, GIVE"

3. PREPARATION VERBS
   Polish verbs for preparation methods:
   - kchy (27x) = to crush (Polish "kruszyć")
   - schy = to dry (Polish "suszyć")
   - mchy = to wash (Polish "myć")
   - gchy = to heat (Polish "grzać")

4. BODY PARTS
   Polish genitive/case forms for body parts:
   - shey (38x) = of neck (Polish "szyi")
   - oky (36x) = eyes (Polish "oczy")
   - ochey = eyes (variant)
   - rchey = of hand (Polish "ręki")

where a Polish-speaking practitioner uses:
  - Latin for "proper" botanical names
